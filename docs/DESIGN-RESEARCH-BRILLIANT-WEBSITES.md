## 2. What “Brilliant” Design Looks Like (Summary)

Across all three sources, “brilliant” sites consistently show:

- **Clear visual hierarchy** — Headlines first, subtext and CTAs obvious; content ordered by importance.
- **Strong typography** — Distinctive, legible type; often large hero headlines; scale (size/weight) used to guide the eye.
- **Generous whitespace** — Prevents overload; improves scannability and a premium feel.
- **Purpose-led layout** — Navigation and sections support the main goal (e.g. e‑commerce → shop, portfolio → work).
- **Trust and clarity** — Social proof, testimonials, logos, clear CTAs; complex topics (compliance, security, AI) made approachable.
- **Consistent identity** — Color, type, and tone aligned with brand and audience (e.g. tech/digital vs. editorial vs. warm/D2C).
- **Modern tech feel (when relevant)** — Gradients, soft motion, clean UI, optional dark/neon accents for digital/tech products.

Patterns that show up repeatedly:

- **Hero:** One clear message + one primary CTA; sometimes no copy (image-only) for maximum impact.
- **Color:** Either calm/neutral (creams, soft blues, pastels) or bold (gradients, neon on dark, strong accent).
- **Motion:** Subtle (scroll, hover, light animation) and often respect for reduced motion.
- **Mobile:** Responsive, touch-friendly, simplified nav and hierarchy on small screens.

## 4. Design & Technology Patterns (from Tabnav and fetched sites)

### Typography

- **Hero:** Often one bold headline (left-aligned or centered); sometimes split (title left, image/video right).
- **Fonts:** Mix of custom (CohereText, Super Sans, Basiersquare, Geist Sans) and system/standard (Inter, Space Grotesk, Roboto, system-ui). Serif for editorial/premium (Lora, PPMori, Styrene).
- **Scale:** Large hero type; smaller subtext and body; clear heading levels.

### Color

- **Calm / trust:** Soft blues, creams, pastels, light gradients (Synthesia, Portal, Cluely, Quite Nice).
- **Tech / digital:** Dark backgrounds + neon or bright accent (Huly, Blync AI, Cleanmeter, Evervault); navy + cyan/green common.
- **Bold / brand:** Strong gradients (purple, magenta, red); Koffiracha (neon yellow + black); Contentsquare (red).

### Layout

- **Hero:** Single message + primary CTA; sometimes image-only or video-only.
- **Sections:** Clear blocks (features, social proof, product, FAQ); cards and grids common.
- **Navigation:** Minimal top nav; commercial vs non-commercial placement (e.g. Allbirds); sometimes sticky or hover-reveal.

### Motion & effects

- **Common:** GSAP, Framer Motion, Lottie; scroll-linked effects; light hover states.
- **3D / immersive:** Three.js (Giga, Falter); Canvas (Sound Of Change, Inra).
- **Reduced motion:** Not always mentioned; best practice is to respect `prefers-reduced-motion`.

### Tech stack (mentioned)

- **CMS:** WordPress, Webflow, Sanity, DatoCMS, HubSpot, Shopify.
- **Animations:** GSAP, Framer Motion, Lottie, Emotion, Three.js.
- **Fonts:** Custom webfonts + system fallbacks.

## 6. Recommendations for Our Project (MSRD)

- **Hierarchy:** One clear hero line per page; primary CTA obvious; secondary/legal in footer.
- **Typography:** Strong headline scale; readable body; consider one distinctive display font for hero.
- **Whitespace:** Keep sections uncluttered; use spacing to separate “trust” vs “action” vs “learn more.”
- **Trust:** Logos, testimonials, compliance/security mentions (e.g. SOC2, secure auth) where relevant.
- **Color (if moving to navy + neon):** Use navy as base (e.g. sections, header/footer); neon (cyan/green) for CTAs, links, key highlights; keep contrast and readability (WCAG). Reference: Huly (dark neon), Cleanmeter (dark + warm accents), Neon (tech clarity).
- **Map:** Can stay light (e.g. Voyager) or use a subtle dark tint that matches navy; ensure neon accents (e.g. pins, borders) don’t clash.
- **Motion:** Subtle scroll/hover; respect `prefers-reduced-motion`; avoid heavy animation on every page.
- **Inner pages:** Same gradient or background system (e.g. page-gradient) and same nav/footer so the whole site feels one product.
